Programs

Give your children the gift of a lifetime- learning a foreign language! Programs are available year-round! We have organized many activities and independent field trips to explore some of the nearby cultural and historic points of interest, and to help you understand the culture and heritage of Mexico.

Why learn Spanish?

With over 35 million Spanish speakers in the United States, the U.S. has the 5th largest Spanish-speaking population. In today's diverse work environment, speaking Spanish is always preferred and often required as part of many careers. Learning Spanish will open many doors and provide you with an instant competitive advantage. Due to the similarity between English and Spanish words and because written Spanish is almost completely phonetic, it is one of the easiest foreign languages to learn. Improve skills to communicate in multiple languages in order to devise and exchange ideas clearly and effectively. Acquire knowledge and critical appreciation of the diversity of human cultural and social experiences.

Our Program

Make your next family trip a learning vacation! Experience the beautiful city of Guanajuato, Mexico while simultaneously enriching your children’s lives with language and Mexican culture!

Immerse your child in a new language and give them an experience they will never forget. Guanajuato is an excellent location to immerse your children in the Spanish language. It is extremely safe and child-friendly. Most of the city is accessible by foot. Many of our host families can also accommodate entire families- the perfect way to complement your children’s Spanish studies! Many families also have children that either live in the home, or visit frequently, so it will give your child more opportunities to play in Spanish!

Besides their Spanish classes during the day, children will have the opportunity to participate in many other activities. Kids love our visits to museums with teachers, craft activities such as piñata and Mexican-kite making, a visit to the largest market in Guanajuato, city-scavenger hunts and weekend trips to nearby cities and towns. The large range of activities outside of our school will not only help your children apply the language they learn in class in a real-world context, but also give them a chance to experience Mexican culture, history, and traditions first-hand. Programs are offered for six weeks for the children between the age group of 12 to 18. Following are the programs offered :

Beginning Spanish

Introduces the children to basic phrases, pronunciation, alphabets, numbers, articles and pronouns, days, months, year, directions, colors and family in Spanish

Advanced Spanish

This course introduces students to fundamentals of Spanish, with emphasis on developing listening, comprehension and speaking skills. It includes activities for developing abilities in speaking, listening, reading, and writing.

Conversational Spanish

We'll read several dialogs that will teach you how to ask for and tell time in Spanish, as well as talking about days of the week and dates in general . We'll also review the essential vocabulary and expressions from the dialog.

Advanced Conversational Spanish

This course provides extensive practice in oral communication, with emphasis on the development of oral proficiency and skills such as discussion, reading aloud, story telling, informative presentations, and persuasive speaking. It includes a review of grammar and usage in the context of the spoken word.

Spanish Grammar and Composition

This course covers an extensive review of complex grammatical structures with vocabulary building. Students will do a selected grammar review to strengthen skills in Spanish language composition. The basic writing skills of spelling, punctuation, and accentuation will also be reviewed. Differences between the written and spoken language will be pointed out toward the production of compositions using the written standard.

Cultural Treasures of Mexico

Studying the arts, culture, and history of central Mexico creates the context for the program. Living with carefully selected Mexican families helps State students develop their language skills while experiencing firsthand the cultural treasures of Mexico. This course is a study of the customs, ideas, and attitudes of the Spanish-speaking peoples. It explores the development of regional differences in popular culture, as well as the changes in the material and intellectual culture of ruling groups.
